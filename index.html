Getting There

G

Flying to RNO

The Reno airport is a scenic one-hour drive from Tahoe City, CA. Rental cars and shuttles are available at the airport. The next closest major airport is Sacramento, a two-hour drive through the Tahoe National Forest.

Flying to SFO or OAK

If you're interested in enjoying the Bay Area before the wedding weekend, we recommend flying to SFO or OAK. The drive to Tahoe city will take 3-4 hours depending on traffic.

Accommodations

G

Hotels

Sunnyside Lodge and Granlibakken Resort are within 5 minutes of the wedding site. The Hyatt Regency, Ritz Carlton, and Squaw Valley are within 30 minutes.

Estates

Groups may be interested in reserving one of North Lake Tahoe's beautiful estates. We suggest searching Airbnb, Homeaway, Flipkey, and VRBO for reservations.

Inns

There are many more inns and bed & breakfasts in North Lake Tahoe. The Cottage Inn and the Chinquapin are both near Tahoe City.

Activities

G

Boating & Beaches

Boats, jetskis, kayaks, paddleboards, and more are available at both Commons Beach and Kings Beach. We highly recommend a day of tubing around the lake, an icy swim in Emerald Bay, and some early morning sportfishing.

Truckee River

Lake Tahoe's only outlet is the Truckee River on the northwestern side of the lake. Be sure to take advantage of the area's spectacular hikes, float trips, mountain biking, fishing, and golf.

Fireworks

The Independence Day celebrations start a day early at Kings Beach. On July 3rd, you can head over to the North Tahoe Event Center at 2pm for food, music, and fireworks. On the 4th of July, Tahoe City's annual Independence Day Celebration will take place at Commons Beach. Fireworks begin at dusk and can be enjoyed from the beach or out on the water.
